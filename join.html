While these are unpaid roles, development hardware and software is available for checkout. All code contributions are made though GitHub. This can allow you to build up a portfolio to present for future opportunities.

For Developer roles, it is very helpful to have a computer science, engineering, or math background.

Because development is done with Object-Oriented languages, knowledge of object-oriented programming (OOP) is a prerequisite for the Development roles. Most modern programming languages such as Java, C#, Objective-C, Swift, and C++ are all object-oriented. Thus, knowledge of OOP is essential.

Training is available for those without prior programming or OOP experience.

Rotations

To allow individuals to experience different disciplines, Developer roles are available on a seasonal basis: Winter, Spring, Summer, Fall. Developers are encouraged to explore other development roles seasonally.

What You Can Expect

What you can expect is an experience where you will be exposed to industry-used software development processes and tools. You can also expect to experience industry-like tasks that are similar in depth and difficulty of what you can experience in an internship at a technology company.

Proof-of-Concept Methodology

Most projects are planned for release on multiple platforms in order reach as wide an audience as possible. The most commonly used platforms include Universal Windows Platform (UWP), Android, iOS, and OSX.
Before or during announcement of each project, a running model or hi-fi prototype will be implemented on one of the platforms.
This will ensure that each project can be implemented with as many potentially blocking issues identified and resolved before porting to other platforms begins.
While unforeseen, platform-specific issues can still cause blocking, algorithmic issues should all be resolved by the model or hi-fi prototype.
